1. System Overview

Microcontroller

  • ESP32-WROOM-32E module
  • WiFi + Bluetooth, 4 MB flash, dual-core 240 MHz
  • USB-C programming via CH340C UART bridge with auto-reset

Power

  • 24 VAC input — single 2-position screw terminal (J_PWR)
  • L1 and L2 independently fused (500 mA each)
  • Separate 1 A fuse on L1 → relay contact common bus
  • On-board: ~33 V DC → 5 V buck → 3.3 V LDO

Relay Outputs

  • 8 × SPST-NO relays, 5 V coil, active HIGH
  • Driven by 8-channel Darlington array
  • All relay contact commons bussed to 24 VAC L1 via dedicated 1 A fuse
  • 8-position 3.5 mm screw terminal (J_ZONES, Z1–Z8)
  • MOV snubber across each output contact

User Controls

  • 8-pos raised DIP switch — “Zones” — via I²C expander
  • 4-pos BCD rotary switch — “Time” — PCB-mount flat-blade slot
  • Illuminated push button — “Man. Start” — board-mounted

Board Topology

  • PCB split into two zones by a 25 mm case-divider band
  • Electronics zone: relays, ESP32, power supply, UART
  • Terminal zone: screw terminals, DIP switch, rotary, button

Enclosure

  • Split plastic case (ABS/PC, UL94 V-0 preferred)
  • Upper cover: electronics zone — closed
  • Lower cover: terminal zone — open front, accessible
  • Internal divider wall aligns with PCB 25 mm band

3. Power Architecture

3.1 Input Terminal

Single 2-position 3.5 mm screw terminal (J_PWR). No barrel jack. No power-select jumper.

TerminalLabelConnection
J_PWR pin 124 VAC L1F1 (circuit power) in series; F3 (relay contact commons) also taps L1 — both fuses are independent
J_PWR pin 224 VAC L2F2 (circuit power return) in series — same electrical node as J_COM (output common return)

3.2 Fusing

Three independent fuses. Do not share or combine. A fault on the output side (F3) must not affect the electronics supply (F1).

3.4 Common Return Topology

J_PWR pin 2 (24 VAC L2) and J_COM are the same electrical node. Only one physical terminal is needed for both the AC return and the output common return. Label silkscreen COM / L2. Two screw positions provided on J_COM for convenient field wiring.

4. Relay Output Circuit

4.1 Driver

8-channel Darlington array (U5) drives relay coils from 3.3 V ESP32 GPIOs. Active HIGH — GPIO HIGH energises relay.

  • Inputs 1–8 → GPIO4, 13, 14, 25, 26, 27, 32, 33 (relay channels 1–8)
  • COM pin (pin 10) must connect to +5 V — flyback diode return rail
  • GND pin (pin 9) to GND

4.2 Relay Contact Wiring

NetDescription
ZONE_PWR_BUS24 VAC L1 → F3 (1 A) → relay K1–K8 contact COM pins (all 8 bussed)
ZONE_1 … ZONE_8Relay K1–K8 NO contacts → J_ZONES Z1–Z8
ZONE_COM_RETJ_COM (24 VAC L2) — load return

4.3 Zone Output Terminal

Single 8-position 3.5 mm screw terminal block (J_ZONES), positions labeled Z1–Z8. Placed at the terminal-zone edge of the board.

4.4 MOV Snubbers

One 47 V MOV disc (RV1–RV8) across each relay NO contact and its common. Inductive load transient protection. Place as close as possible to J_ZONES.

6. I²C GPIO Expander

An MCP23008 (8-bit I²C GPIO expander) reads all 8 DIP switch inputs, freeing ESP32 GPIOs for other functions. The I²C bus uses GPIO21 (SDA) and GPIO22 (SCL) with 4.7 kΩ pull-ups to 3.3 V on each line.

6.1 Wiring

MCP23008 PinConnection
VDD3.3 V
GNDGND
SCL (pin 1)ESP32 GPIO22 — 4.7 kΩ pull-up to 3.3 V
SDA (pin 2)ESP32 GPIO21 — 4.7 kΩ pull-up to 3.3 V
A0, A1, A2 (pins 3–5)All to GND → I²C address 0x20
RESET (pin 6)3.3 V (active LOW — tie HIGH for normal operation)
GP0–GP7 (pins 10–17)SW_DIP positions 1–8. Other side of each switch to GND. No external pull-ups needed — enable via GPPU register in firmware.

6.2 Firmware Note

  • IODIR register (0x00) = 0xFF — all pins input
  • GPPU register (0x06) = 0xFF — all internal pull-ups enabled
  • Read GPIO register (0x09) — returns one byte; bit 0 = Zone 1 … bit 7 = Zone 8; 0 = switch ON (closed to GND)

6.3 Package

Use SOIC-18 for JLCPCB SMD assembly. Confirm LCSC part number and footprint before ordering.

9. PCB Requirements

9.1 Board Zones

Critical dimension: The terminal zone must extend 25 mm beyond the electronics zone. This clearance fits the internal case divider wall. No components within 5 mm of the divider boundary on either side.
  • Mark the electronics / terminal boundary on the silkscreen with a dashed line
  • All screw terminals (J_PWR, J_ZONES, J_COM), SW_DIP, SW_ROT, and SW_START within the terminal zone
  • All relay bodies, ESP32 module, and power supply ICs within the electronics zone

9.2 Layer Stack and General Rules

  • 2-layer PCB minimum. 4-layer if routing density requires.
  • ESP32 antenna keep-out: 15 mm copper-free zone on all layers around module antenna end
  • LM2596 switching loop: keep tight, no signal traces in that region
  • GND flood fill both layers, via-stitched. Star ground between AC rectifier section and DC logic GND.
  • ULN2803A COM pin (pin 10) must connect to +5 V — do not leave floating
  • GPIO12: 10 kΩ pull-down to GND, no other connections
  • USB-C CC1 and CC2 pins: 5.1 kΩ to GND each
  • Auto-reset: Q1 base → CH340C RTS# via 10 kΩ + 100 nF to GND; Q2 base → CH340C DTR# via 10 kΩ + 100 nF to GND
